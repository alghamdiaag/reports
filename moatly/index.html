Where the Market Fails the Founder
Moment 1
The founder doesn't know what to protect first — and the wrong choice costs them the asset that actually mattered
The founder knows they have something worth protecting. They just don't know exactly what it is. Is it the name? The code? The idea? The method? They sit in front of their screen trying to figure out where to start — worried they'll pay to protect the wrong thing while the real asset stays exposed.

This moment — the silence before any action — is the most costly of all. Because it happens before everything else. And every wrong decision here compounds later.
Critical Gap Cannot determine whether their technical feature is protectable — or whether someone got there first ▾
What they lose
Weeks — sometimes months — building a feature believed to be proprietary, only to discover a similar patent already exists. The wrong call at this moment wipes out all development effort.
What exists today
USPTO and WIPO databases are complex and require legal expertise to interpret. Attorneys bill by the hour. No tool simplifies this search for the non-legal founder.
What the market is waiting for
Reduce the time needed to determine whether a technical feature or brand name is protectable — from weeks to hours — without requiring prior legal expertise
Source: Digital research — PatentPC, WIPO, SVB Startup Insights · 4+ independent sources
Critical Gap Overlooks protecting a critical competitive asset because they didn't realize it was protectable in the first place ▾
What they lose
A competitor files for what the founder built before them — because the founder didn't know it was registrable. That loss is permanent once the other party registers first.
What exists today
No guided tool walks founders through their own work and flags: "What you just built — did you know this can be registered?" Available educational content is generic and non-contextual.
What the market is waiting for
Reduce the likelihood of missing protection for a core competitive asset — through automatic discovery that runs alongside product development, not after it
Source: Digital research — Mintz IP Guide, SVB, Nyemaster · 5+ sources
Moment 4 · Highest Risk
The trademark conflict surfaces after launch — after the entire brand budget has already been spent
The company launches. The brand looks strong. Users engage. Then the legal notice arrives. Another company owns a matching or similar trademark — registered three years earlier — in the same market.

This moment kills investment deals during Due Diligence. The investor sees the legal exposure and doesn't close. The brand gets rebuilt from scratch. Time and money are lost — for a problem that could have been caught in hours before launch.
Critical Gap · Top Priority Discovers a trademark conflict after launch and after investing in brand identity ▾
What they lose
A complete brand rebuild — name, logo, domain, social handles, marketing materials — after money has already been spent. This can stop an investment deal at an advanced stage.
What exists today
TESS is available for manual review — but it's complex, requires knowledge of the correct trademark classes, and doesn't cover multiple markets simultaneously. Many founders don't know it exists until it's too late.
What the market is waiting for
Reduce the time needed to check for trademark conflicts across multiple markets — before any brand-building decision — from days to minutes, without legal expertise
Source: Digital research — WIPO, Mintz, SVB, Nyemaster "A1 Motorcycles" case · 6+ sources
Moment 8
The registration lapses silently — and the founder doesn't find out until months after the deadline passed
The filing is complete. The company is growing. The team is focused on product and sales. Then on an ordinary day, a notice arrives from the IP office: the renewal deadline has passed. The registration is cancelled. It cannot be reinstated.

What was built over years — falls because of a missed deadline. And nothing is harder to explain to an investor than that your competitive moat lapsed because you missed an email.
Critical Gap Loses an active registration by missing a renewal deadline — an unrecoverable loss ▾
What they lose
In many jurisdictions, registration cannot be reinstated after the grace period. The loss is permanent — and can derail an investment round at an advanced stage.
What exists today
Email notifications from IP offices — arriving inside a crowded inbox. No independent alert system systematically tracks deadlines across multiple registrations and markets.
What the market is waiting for
Reduce the likelihood of losing an active registration to a missed deadline — with a tiered alert system (90, 30, 7 days) for every registration in every market, requiring zero manual action
Source: Digital research — USPTO Renewal Policy, upcounsel.com, FasterCapital · 5+ sources
